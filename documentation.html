Art Nouveau Style

A Brief Overview

The Art Nouveaustyle employed in this website draws inspiration from the late 19th-century artistic movement, particularly the works of Czech artist Alphonse Mucha.

Art Nouveau, also known as the Modern Style, emerged as a significant artistic movement between 1890 and 1910. It aimed to establish a wholly unique style that diverged from previous artistic eras, breaking away from imitation.

Key Features of Art Nouveau:

Asymmetry: Art Nouveau embraces asymmetry, avoiding the rigid symmetry of previous styles.

Plenty of Female Images: Art Nouveau is characterized by an abundance of female images. This aligns seamlessly with our website's focus on portraying women's images.

Sinuous Lines: It features sinuous, flowing lines inspired by forms found in nature.

Decorative Organic Forms: The style incorporates decorative elements derived from animals, flowers, and plants.


Typography

FONTS:

Primary Font - Rochester: The primary font, used for titles and the navbar.

Our selection of the 'Rochester' font for our Art Nouveau-inspired project draws inspiration from the Art Nouveau movement's characteristic features. While Art Nouveau didn't establish specific fonts, it left a distinct mark on typography, emphasizing curved and organic shapes and a penchant for high ornamentation. The 'Rochester' font aligns with these principles, with its flowing lines, ornate details, and elegant script.

Ver Sacrum illustration by Koloman Moser (1899)

By choosing 'Rochester,' we aim to capture the essence of Art Nouveau and infuse our design with a sense of vintage elegance and sophistication. While the font may not be a direct product of the era, it serves as a bridge between the past and the present, allowing us to create a visually pleasing and harmonious result. It's our way of paying homage to Art Nouveau's timeless charm while giving our project a contemporary twist.

Colors

In the Art Nouveau style, particularly exemplified by Alphonse Mucha's iconic works, color plays a pivotal role.he likes to use muted and earthy tones, adds delicate and gentle accents to his works, and usually draws inspiration from the natural world. he also likes to use Gold and Metallic Accents to enhance the luxurious and ornate quality, to highlight specific elements.

Here are chosen colors for the style:

The color palette employed in the website design aims to evoke a sense of softness and harmony. The primary background color, Light Pink (#f9efea), blankets the entire website, enveloping it in a gentle and soothing ambiance. Complementing this, various light shades are strategically integrated to maintain a soft and inviting tone throughout the site.

To add depth and contrast, a range of different grey hues is artfully woven into the design. These greys create a harmonious balance with the other colors, providing visual interest while preserving an overall sense of tranquility.

Notably, a touch of gold is introduced for the 'hr' element, paying homage to the golden threads found in books of the Art Nouveau era. This subtle gilded detail adds a touch of elegance and historical reference to the design, enriching the overall aesthetic.


Layout and others

LAYOUT:

Incorporating nature-inspired elements is a hallmark of Art Nouveau. In our design, we utilize floral and plant imagery to enhance the overall aesthetics:

Floral Borders: Flower and plant images are elegantly incorporated as borders for dropdown menus, lending a decorative and organic touch.

Cursor: The cursor is designed to resemble a flower, adding a unique and thematic element that aligns seamlessly with the Art Nouveau style.

Female Images: Alphonse Mucha's iconic female images are featured as covers and button images, personifying glamour, modernity, and beauty, which perfectly aligns with the Art Nouveau theme.

Bauhaus Style

A Brief Overview

The Bauhaus movment as been a pivotal artistic movement in the history of western Art. Started as a way for young artis to evade the costrains of accademic art and to express a renovate sense of freedom, Bauhaus aim is to bring a renovate artistic tast in the ramping society of earli XX Century. Officially started in 1919, the movement call for a comprehensive and sparse approace to the art, because of this a large number of people started to experiment different forms of art and supports. From painting to architecture, from waving to teather, the Bauhaus movement has shaped the beginning of what we now call modern society.

Since the topics of this project are centred on the retelling of women perspective in the Greek mythology, we found appropriate to use a similar approach in the choice of the graphic elements. Because of this we dig a bit in the wast production of the Bauhaus-related artis and we found the works of Gunta Stölzl particularly interesting for our project. Gunta Stölzl has been a German textile artist whose works not only represent a perfect example of Bauhaus innovation, but also played a foundamental role in the transposition of visual art into the realm of modern industrial design.

Since the homepage has to introduce the nature of the project, we display two of Gunta Stölzl most representative of Bauhaus-inspired work, namely an experimental design fo a carpet and a painting . The images in the "issue " section off the homepage are display a photographic portrait of Gunta Stölzl, amnd one of her late drawing . The page dedicated to the "Death" issue once again display an artwork of Gunta Stölzl, but this time it is a draft drawing showing the unfolding of the artist's creative process.

In the 'love' issue we decide to display an artwork of Paul Klee titled 'the lovers', this painting better fit in the atmosphere of the greek conception of love we discuss, as well it give us the opportunity to Better represent the Bauhaus style beyond the production of a single artist.

Typography

FONTS:

For what concerns the choice of the font and text styling, we adopted the closest once we find similar to that used in the Bauhaus manifestos publications around 1919. Waving but regular shape are the main characteristics of the typing styles of these period, when rounded shape reflected the innovation of the bauhaus, especially in a high formalised context such as publishing. Even if it was typical for these publication to display the text in a deconstructed and irregular shape, we decide not to imitated these design, since it would have turned into hard to read paragraphs and a messy visual effect. Instead we kept an ordered square disposition that should mirror those in Gunta Stölzl textile production.

Late XX Century Style

A Brief Overview

For the Late XX century theme, we choose to emulate a mid 90s famous website, namely Ebay (at that time still call Auctionebay) that was lunched in 1995. Since the development of graphics for the web was really poor at that time, instead of just copy the website appearance, we pick up the most relevant characteristics and try to reshape them in a more pleasant way. We identified the more peculiar feature of this period as a really hight color contrast between tex and background, specifically in the grey-black/green-blak range, as well as a very pixel-shaped font resembling typewriter printing. Text disposition is square-organised and really homogeneous. Image decoration was basically absent in the original website, nonetheless we took the license to add a couple of images; in other not to change the overall idea of an old website, we choose pixel-art-shaped minimal logos for the issues headers and buttons.

Cyberpunk Style

A Brief Overview

The Cyberpunk style is a visually captivating and immersive digital aesthetic inspired by the dystopian and futuristic worlds often depicted in the Cyberpunk genre, notably popularized by games like Cyberpunk 2077. This style combines edgy typography, glitch effects, and a neon-infused color palette to create a visually striking and immersive user experience.

Layout and others

The Cyberpunk style follows a grid-based layout with a focus on creating a futuristic and immersive visual experience. Here's an overview of the layout elements:

Glitched HR: The glitched horizontal rule, or HR, serves as a unique decorative element that adds a touch of digital distortion and movement to the design.

Buttons: Glitch-style buttons with hover effects are used throughout the style to encourage user interaction and engagement.

Issue Cards: Issue cards use bold typography, color highlights, and glitch effects to draw attention to key content. These cards effectively convey information about various topics within the Cyberpunk theme.
